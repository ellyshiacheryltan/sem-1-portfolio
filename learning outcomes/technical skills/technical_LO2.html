Before I started with the implementation of my portfolio website, I started off first with sketching my ideas on how I want my website layout to look like. Then after sketching I asked for feedback from one of my teachers, also to know if I can continue to the next step: wireframing, then asked for feedback again before I start with the prototyping. Reflection

Through this work I learned how to do an iterative process of ideation before going to the implementation of the product, and I also learned how to make an interactive prototype to give potential-user a visualization on how my product (this portfolio wwebsite) will look like.

Group Project Wireframing to Prototyping

(Click to see more)

After we're done with the paper prototype and testing, we moved on to improving the layout more to have a clearer visualisation of how we want the website design to look like, by making a wireframe. Then we tested the wireframe too on 5 people to see if our design is user friendly, and if the user can navigate well through our website. Here is the wireframe that we made:




From the testing we found that our design is user-friendly and easy-to-navigate enough, so we proceeded to making the interactive prototype. During the implementation, this prototype was already modified a few times based on the feedbacks that we got from our peers and teachers through user testing. After a few iterative processes we finally finalized the design which can be seen here in our final interactive prototype:


Reflection

Through this iterative process I learned that I always have to take into account, and prioritize the target users' interests and inputs to make a product that is actually going to be beneficial and useful for them. I also learned that I always have to be open to making changes, even changes that I did not like, in order to satisfy the users' needs.
